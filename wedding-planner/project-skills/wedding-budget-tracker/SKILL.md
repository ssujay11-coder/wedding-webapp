## Budget Tracking Workflow

1. **Set Total Budget**
   - Determine available funds
   - Identify funding sources (couple, parents, other)
   - Set realistic total budget

2. **Allocate by Category**
   - Use percentage guidelines
   - Adjust based on priorities
   - Example: Photography lovers allocate 15-20% to photo/video

3. **Research and Estimate**
   - Get quotes from vendors
   - Input estimated costs
   - Note quote expiration dates

4. **Book and Track**
   - Update with actual costs when booked
   - Record deposits paid
   - Track payment due dates

5. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - Weekly budget review
   - Reallocate if over in one category
   - Identify cost-saving opportunities

6. **Final Reconciliation**
   - Compare estimated vs actual
   - Track final payments
   - Analyze for future reference

## Cost-Saving Strategies

### Venue & Catering
- Off-peak season (November-March, except holidays)
- Friday or Sunday instead of Saturday
- Brunch or lunch instead of dinner
- Weekday wedding
- All-inclusive venues
- Limit bar to beer, wine, signature cocktail
- Buffet instead of plated service

### Photography
- Shorter coverage (6 hours vs 10 hours)
- One photographer instead of two
- Digital files only, no album
- Engagement session as add-on only if budget allows

### Flowers & Decor
- In-season flowers
- Greenery-heavy arrangements
- Repurpose ceremony flowers at reception
- DIY centerpieces
- Fewer statement pieces
- Potted plants as centerpieces (guests take home)

### Attire
- Sample sale dresses
- Rent vs buy
- Accessories from non-bridal retailers
- Bridesmaid dresses in budget-friendly colors/styles

### Invitations
- Digital save-the-dates
- Single-card invitations (vs multiple inserts)
- Printable designs (print yourself)
- Simple online RSVP (no physical card)

### Music
- DJ vs live band
- Spotify playlist for cocktail hour
- Friend/family as ceremony musician

### Cake/Desserts
- Smaller display cake, sheet cake for serving
- Dessert bar instead of traditional cake
- Grocery store bakery (surprisingly good!)
- Cupcakes or donuts

### General Strategies
- Smaller guest list = biggest savings
- DIY decorations and favors
- Borrow vs buy (card box, unity candle, etc.)
- Digital programs instead of printed
- Prioritize what matters most, cut what doesn't

## Hidden Costs to Remember

### Often Forgotten Expenses
- **Service charges & gratuity**: 18-22% on catering
- **Sales tax**: 5-10% on most services
- **Vendor meals**: $20-30 per vendor
- **Overtime fees**: If reception runs over
- **Delivery fees**: Cake, flowers, rentals
- **Setup/breakdown fees**: Decor, rentals
- **Parking fees**: Valet or lot rental
- **Coat check**: Cold weather weddings
- **Postage**: Invitations are often over 1 stamp
- **Hotel room**: Night before and wedding night
- **Alterations**: Dress and suit tailoring
- **Undergarments**: Special shapewear, shoes
- **Marriage license**: $30-100
- **Officiant fee**: $50-500
- **Guest book & pen**: $20-50
- **Card box**: $15-40
- **Cake knife/server set**: $25-75
- **Rehearsal dinner**: $500-2,000
- **Day-after brunch**: $300-1,000

## Budget Tracking Best Practices

- **Update weekly**: Review and update budget every week
- **Save all receipts**: Keep physical and digital copies
- **Track cash expenses**: Easy to forget cash tips and small purchases
- **Build in 10% buffer**: Contingency for unexpected costs
- **Communicate with partner**: Both should have access to budget
- **Use budget app or spreadsheet**: Centralized tracking
- **Set spending alerts**: Notification when approaching category limit
- **Prioritize before booking**: Know your top 3 priorities

## Export Formats

- **Excel/Google Sheets**: Full interactive spreadsheet
- **PDF Summary**: One-page budget overview
- **CSV**: Raw data for import elsewhere
- **Visual Report**: Charts and graphs
- **Payment Schedule**: Timeline view of due dates
